I · the preamble

Two names I have learned to say like prayers.

m i m i  ·  x y l a

Before either of you, I thought I knew the words for the good things. Then you each arrived and rearranged the alphabet. Now every letter I write is really just one of two.

II · xyla, in polaroids

The girl who arrived and made ordinary an impossible word.

Before you I had never noticed how many times a day the light changes. Now I watch for it — because watching the light is how I find your face again each morning.

Curls like handwriting. Eyes that ask the room what's next. A laugh that makes strangers turn around and smile at nothing in particular.

I have a thousand of these moments stored somewhere behind my ribs. These five are just the ones I could fit on the page.

III · for mimi

The quiet reason the loud, happy things happen at all.

mine.
always.

I found the world the day I found you. The rest has just been learning where to put things.

You carry our daughter like a hymn you already know by heart — and the room goes warm and I remember why I said yes.

If love is a language, yours has fewer words and better sentences. I will spend the rest of my life learning your grammar.

Thank you for making the ordinary Tuesday feel like something worth framing.

a recurring dream of mine

When you are older, we'll ride into any century you ask for.

I will build you a future wide enough for dinosaurs. I will keep the reins steady, and the dust out of your eyes. You point — we go.
